# LadderIQ Design Philosophy

1. **The investor remains in control.** AI recommends; the user approves.
2. **Every dollar must have a purpose.** Capital is allocated by role, opportunity, and risk.
3. **Great company does not equal great buy today.** Quality and opportunity are separate.
4. **Harvest strength; accumulate planned weakness.** Do not chase or panic.
5. **Measure the process, not just the outcome.** A lucky trade can still be bad process.
6. **Benchmark everything.** Complexity must earn its keep against QQQ and buy-and-hold.
7. **External cash flows are not investment returns.** Performance calculations must segment contributions and withdrawals.
8. **Learning requires evidence.** One override is an observation, not a new rule.
9. **No silent strategy drift.** Proposed rule changes require review, confidence, and rollback capability.
10. **Explain every recommendation.** The user should know what changed, why, and what evidence supports it.
11. **Fail unsafe builds.** Invalid ladders should stop publication rather than appear quietly in the dashboard.
12. **Preserve the product's memory.** Decisions, versions, overrides, and outcomes are part of the system.
