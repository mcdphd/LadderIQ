# LadderIQ History

## Phase 0 — Manual ladder discipline

The project began as a practical system for staging buys and sells rather than making emotional all-or-nothing decisions. The early objective was simple: define prices, quantities, cash limits, and review cadence before the market moved.

## Phase 1 — Portfolio hierarchy

Holdings were separated into strategic core, tactical compounders, growth engines, rotation candidates, and special situations. This prevented every stock from receiving equal capital and established role-based portfolio management.

## Phase 2 — Leadership and rotation

The leadership scanner introduced trend, relative strength, momentum, and market-mode logic. Strategic-core safeguards were added so one weak reading could not automatically demote a long-term holding.

## Phase 3 — Portfolio Command Center

The system evolved from scripts and tables into a dashboard with portfolio KPIs, decision cards, buy and sell ladders, leadership profiles, and capital-allocation guidance.

## Phase 4 — Opportunity vs. quality

A major conceptual correction separated current Opportunity Score from long-term Business Quality. NVDA exposed the flaw: a world-class business could have a low current deployment score because it was overweight or technically weak.

## Phase 5 — Active, approved, and legacy capital

The architecture separated Active Holdings, Approved Universe, and Legacy Holdings. Employer stock such as BAH could now be evaluated under transition rules without being treated as a normal LadderIQ selection.

## Phase 6 — Benchmarking and replay

QQQ comparison, date sliders, buy-and-hold benchmarks, ladder alpha, and value-added concepts were introduced. This shifted the product from merely producing trades to proving whether the process added value.

## Phase 7 — Cash-flow-aware performance

Additional contributions exposed the weakness of a single static ROI denominator. Capital events and time-weighted return were introduced so gains earned before a contribution remained measured against the capital actually at work during that period.

## Phase 8 — Adaptive intelligence

Manual overrides such as NVDA micro-harvesting and AMZN sell-spacing adjustments became learning events. The system direction changed from static rules to transparent, human-approved adaptation.

## Current phase — Product discipline

The project is formalizing semantic versioning, documentation, release controls, centralized templates, auditability, and QA gates so features do not regress between builds.
